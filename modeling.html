Core Modeling Workflows

Explore the three pillars of modern 3D creation. From industrial precision to organic digital clay, learn the techniques that power the digital world.

Get Started
01. Rigid Construction

Polygonal Modeling

The most fundamental method of creating 3D objects using straight lines and flat surfaces. It is the industry standard for real-time applications and video games.

  • Core Components Relies on vertices (points), edges (connecting lines), and facets (polygonal surfaces).
  • Geometric Primitives Uses cubes, spheres, and cylinders as building blocks to construct complex shapes.
  • Boolean Modeling Uses logical operators like Union, Intersection, and Difference for precise cutouts and combinations.
  • Derivative Techniques Includes extrusion (pulling faces) and revolve (spinning profiles) to generate 3D volumes.
02. Organic Free-Form

Digital Sculpting

Focuses on techniques designed for creating organic, irregular, or soft shapes. It simulates the tactile experience of working with physical clay.

Virtual Sculpting

Artists push and pull meshes as if they were clay.

Blobby Surfaces

Soft forms such as liquids or clouds.

Lattice Deformation

Smooth deformation of high-resolution models.

Natural Terrains

Simulating eroded mountains and landscapes.

03. Mathematical Precision

NURBS Modeling

NURBS (Non-Uniform Rational B-Splines) focus on mathematical representations of curves for industrial-grade precision and perfectly smooth surfaces.

  • Weighted Control Shapes are defined by control points and weights.
  • Bezier Curves High-precision lines defined by tangent handles.
  • Perfect Splines Resolution-independent curved lines.
  • CAD Standards Essential for manufacturing and aerospace.
